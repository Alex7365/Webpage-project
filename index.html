Important information about rabbits

We have covered some facts about the cool abilities of rabbits. Now we will talk about the pros and cons of having a rabbit so you can decide if a rabbit is the right pet for you.

1. Rabbits are prey animals

Rabbits are prey animals which means they are being hunted by other animals such as eagles, foxes, and snakes. This means they are scared of other animals that are bigger than them. If you have a cat or a dog this could result in the rabbit being scared in your house. This does not mean you can't have a rabbit in a dog but it does mean that you will have to keep an eye on your rabbit until they get to know each other.

2. Rabbits are expensive

When we say a rabbit is expensive we do not mean that they want the finest carrots or lettuce, it means that they are hard to take care of. Rabbits are considered exotic animals which means the medical bill for a rabbit will be high.

3. Rabbits are expensive

You might think that Rabbits are easy to take care of, but in reality Rabbits require special care. Rabbits are considered exotic pets. This means that they need doctors who specialize in rabbits. A yearly check-up can cost from $25 - $55, and an emergency medical need can cost hundreds of dollars.

4. They are can be litter trained

Rabbbits can be litter trained like cats and dogs. To train them first you have to assign a place where they can do their business. Then when they have accidents you can wipe it with a piece of paper and put that paper in the assigned place. Make sure to clean the odor of the place where they did their accident. This will allow them to smell where they did their business and learn the place where they should go.

5. They can adjust to your schedule

Rabbits are smart and can adapt to schedules. If you are worried that your rabbit will wake you up at night or to early, don't worry. Rabbits can adapt to your schedule, it just takes time.

6. Rabbits can learn tricks

Rabbits can learn some tricks. They can learn it just like dogs. Show them how it is done, then when they do it give them a treat, even if it was unintentional. Do it everyday for a few weeks. Then do it without a treat and the will get the hang of it after a while.
